Growing things

FERTILE AND ORGANIC SOILS.

The climate in Sonoma County is perfectly suited to growing all sorts of flowers, fruit trees and vegetables. The gardens are spectacular! There is sun in abundance. Just add water!

Clover is widely used on hillsides and vineyard as legumes for the vines. In the spring, when the red clovers bloom, it looks like the hills are covered with a red carpet that waves easily in the breeze!

California Poppies

Wild poppies, the state flower of California, grow all around the property in the spring. They are a cheerful reminder of the carefree, friendly atmosphere found in Sonoma County!

Tomatoes in basket

Almost all varieties of tomatoes grow easily on the property. Once you've had a home grown organic tomato just ripe off the vine, you'll never look at store-bought tomatoes the same way!

Flowers everywhere

These colorful flowers were planted and watered in full sun. Within three weeks, they were three and a half feet tall, and completely filled the flower beds! This place is amazing!

A rose by any other name...

Roses are a true main stay of Sonoma County. Mediteranean mild climates with lots of sunny, clear skies keep roses blooming all summer long!

French lavender

In Sonoma County, lavender grow like they do in Provence! Here they are use to create a beautiful and orderly hedge to line the driveway, while providing fragrant blooms and lots of beautiful colors in the summer!

Olive flowers

Olives have their flowers in mid spring. The fruits are set and grow slowly in the beautiful summer days. The fruits will rippen and will be ready for harvest in late October.

Something from the 'Down Under'

Kiwis are not native to Sonoma County, but they do well here. Kiwis bloom in clusters and require a pair of male and female vines to bear fruits.

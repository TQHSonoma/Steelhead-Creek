Grapes

Wine grapes have seeds. The skin of the grapes give the wine its color. The tannin of the wine comes from the skin and the seed. Grapes are never washed before crushing. Yes, there are always insects and dirt particles mixed in with the grapes when they are processed. No worries, the alchohol will take care of things naturally. Grapes are harvested when they reach a certain level of sweetness. The sugar content of the grapes is called "Brix". Most red wine grapes are harvested when they reach between 24 and 28 brix (the higher the number, the sweeter). A higher brix will produce a wine with a higher alchohol content. The winemaker typically decide the optimum brix needed before harvesting based on the varietal and the desired results for the wine. While it's easy to make a drinkable wine, making a good wine takes special skills, knowledge, and a little "magic". It's analogous to cooking a good meal at home vs. visiting a Michelin rated restaurant.

Prolific blooms

Blooms become olives. Olive trees do not produce consistenly. Periodically they "rest" for a year before coming back the following year. Typically there are less blooms (and less fruits) in a dry year.

Young but ready

This is a young (5 years old) tree that is ready to produce. Harvesting olives is a completely manual process and is labor intensive.

Wine stuff

Wine making is an art form with a little magic and some science mixed in. A wine press is used to separate the wine from the must. A manual cork machine is used to force the cork into a wine bottle.

Wine making process

First is the harvest of the grapes, and then it's the desteeming and crushing, follow by the cold soak. Then we are on to the primary fermentation with all the required punch downs. When the primary fermentation is complete, then the wine is pressed and separated from the must. The wine is then placed in a barrell (or some other vessels) for secondary fermentation. Then the wine is aged in an oak barrell until it is ready for bottling, corking, and labeling. A lot of work, but a sweet reward in the end
